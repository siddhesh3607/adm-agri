Thursday 14 July 2022

Wheat

  • Markets have firmed on the week, despite a bearish slant to the USDA report, although continued concerns over global inflation and optimism over Black Sea exports have limited the gains.
  • Chicago Dec 22 futures edged up $2.30/t, Matif rose by €17.75/t ($15.48/t) for the same month while London Nov 22 futures increased by £15.30/t ($19.30/t).
  • USDA raised 2022/23 US ending stocks in Tuesday’s report, due to an increase in production. It also raised global inventories when most were expecting a monthly decline.
  • US consumer price index data is expected to show higher inflation in the country, which could force the central bank to increase interest rates.
  • Russia and Ukraine have apparently reached a deal that would open up ‘sea-bound’ export corridors, with plans for Ukrainian vessels to guide ships in and out of mined port waters. In addition, Russia would be urged to respect a truce while shipments move, and Turkey, supported by the UN, would offer to inspect ships to allay Russian fears over weapon smuggling.
  • In the meantime, Ukraine’s Grain Association has raised its 2022 corn and wheat harvest forecast to 27.3 mln t and 20.8 mln t respectively.
  • Romania has re-opened a ‘pre-soviet era’ railway link connecting its Danube river port of Galati to Ukraine a month earlier than expected to help boost vital grain exports.
  • Farmers in Russia’s southern Rostov region are seeing high wheat yields as harvesting gets underway, and expectations are for a repeat of last year’s record crop.
  • European output has been revised down. Strategie Grains has trimmed its 2022/23 EU soft wheat forecast to 123.3 mln t, down 1.1 mln on last month’s estimate and 6.6 mln lower than last year. It stated ‘a delicate equilibrium forecast for EU market in 22/23 with smaller harvests and sustained exports’.
  • In its initial forecast, France’s Agriculture ministry has placed soft wheat production at 32.9 mln t, down 8% year on year, with yields estimated at 6.99t/ha due to rainfall deficits, heat and storm damage.
  • Canadian crops are advancing rapidly amid warmer weather, although sodden fields are seen restricting spray applications forcing some to switch to aerial spraying.
  • Buenos Aires Grain Exchange has again trimmed Argentina’s wheat area forecast, to 6.2 mln ha, down 0.5mln ha compared with last year, with sowings placed at 85% complete.
  • The current UK heatwave is rapidly bringing wheat crops to maturity, although concerns are increasing about the potential impact on yield and quality if the current weather continues.

Malting Barley

  • The first winter malting barley results are coming in and, similar to feed, quality is looking good. In general we are seeing low nitrogen results and high retentions.
  • The hot weather has the potential to hamper the spring crop however, causing it to die off early, possibly to the detriment of yield, grain size and nitrogen.
  • Malting markets are rather quiet, with most customers wanting to get a clear picture of the quality of the crop before they make marketing decisions.
  • European FOB markets are ill defined, with the ongoing volatility in MATIF keeping bid/offer spreads wide.

Feed Barley

  • The winter barley harvest is now well underway. Overall quality is good, with low moistures and high test weights. With the heatwave expected over the weekend, we expect to see the winter harvest wrapped up fairly quickly. Yields overall are approaching 10% above average.
  • The market has chased demand to Spain lower to make room for the incoming harvest, which has added significant harvest pressure against deferred positions, where sellers are more reluctant to discount prices.
  • Overall, demand is rather lacklustre in both domestic and export markets, which is keeping pressure on prices along with softer futures markets.

Rapeseed

  • The trade has focused on USDA’s July world supply and demand estimates, leading Chicago soybeans to trade sharply lower into the close following the report.
  • Whilst US ending stocks were estimated higher for 2021/22, they were lower for 2022/23. USDA kept 2022/23 yields the same, but the recently reported fall in the current area pushed production lower. More importantly, world ending stocks were reported above expectations for 2021/22 at 88.73 mln t, but unchanged on 2022/23.
  • Scattered showers across the Midwest will help crops, but it’s now looking warm and dry for most areas for the next few weeks, which may see the trade add some risk premium back into prices. Crop ratings fell again this week from 63% good/excellent down to 62%.
  • In South America, USDA left production for Brazil unchanged at 126 mln t and increased production in Argentina from 43.4 mln t to 44 mln t.
  • In China, a rise in Covid-19 cases in Shanghai caused more lockdown concerns. Businesses are still unable to reopen and port congestion is building, which may cause further disruption. Chinese demand has dipped sharply recently, with no US soybeans purchased for over six weeks.
  • Oil markets all ended lower again this week. Crude oil fell 8% earlier in the week on fears of recession and falling demand. West Texas Intermediate closed below $96/barrel yesterday for the first time in three months.
  • This, plus rumours that President Putin will turn off Europe’s gas supply this month, coupled with high US inflation and ongoing Chinese Covid-19 cases, was enough for a sell-off.
  • The Malaysian palm oil board estimated June palm yields at 0.25mt/ha higher than expected. As predicted, USDA reduced veg-oil demand given recent global events, increasing world ending stocks for 22/23. Veg oils have traded sharply lower since.
  • MATIF and Canadian canola futures both lowered on the week. There may be concerns in some areas of Canada over the lack of rainfall, which will be closely watched over the coming weeks. MATIF rapeseed followed the complex lower to trade at the lower end of its recent range.
  • EU harvest has started and seed supply looks ample for the next few months, which may pressure prices.

Pulses

Beans

  • A few old crop beans are left on farm, but there is no demand with consumers and shippers covered until new crop.
  • New crop bean prices continue to track wheat futures and will likely do so until harvest commences.
  • Demand for new crop beans is also very limited with consumers both domestically and in Europe happy to sit back and wait for what they expect to be lower prices post-harvest.
  • Egypt has very high stocks of old crop Australian beans, which currently are offered at a discount to the new crop beans from Europe, thus limiting demand for EU beans in the Sept-Dec positions.

Peas

  • Old crop human consumption peas are now complete for the season. The small amount of peas remaining on farm will be heading into the feed market.
  • New crop peas are beginning to near harvesting, with crops across East Anglia and Lincolnshire looking well ahead of the new season. A small amount of rain would not go amiss over the next week, not too much though.
  • Our marrowfat buyback for 2023/2024 has had good uptake since launch, with the first movement period during September and October fully booked. Please get in touch with your farm trader to ensure pre-Christmas movement.

Seed

  • Our winter barley position is now looking very tight. We advise placing orders as soon as possible to avoid disappointment. Yields and quality are good from the seed crops harvested so far.
  • If you are worried about ordering rapeseed and not having the right conditions to drill, consider DUPLO – available on a sale or return deal allowing you to return up to 75% of your seed.
  • Lots of OSR deliveries have begun to take place, so now is the time to be ordering companion crops. We have some great options to choose from to help aid establishment of the OSR. Get in touch with your farm trader for more information.
  • If you are planning on drilling autumn cover crops we have a range of cover crop mixtures available, as well as offering bespoke mixes to suit your requirements.
  • For more information on the varieties we offer, please see our seed varieties information guide here.

Fertiliser

  • India tendered for 500,000t of urea this week, in line with expectations. Results from the tender could highlight limited global urea availability and consolidate current price levels.
  • Europe’s gas crisis continues, and the Nord Stream 1 pipeline from Russia is down for scheduled maintenance. Uncertainties remain over whether the work will be completed in the 10-day window and if gas supplies will recommence at pre-maintenance levels, if at all.
  • Forward gas prices increased for Winter 22 significantly last week, surrounding speculation about future gas flows from Russia. UK ammonium nitrate terms issued Monday morning saw a 13% rise on the back of this.
  • Granular urea in the UK remains excellent value on a £/kg N basis. Slight increases have been seen on the 46N product following ammonium nitrate price increases. Significant upside risk remains for 46N though.
  • PiagranPro, SKW’s urease inhibited urea, remains available for Sept, Oct and Nov delivery, exclusively imported by ADM.
  • Piamon 33N 30SO3, SKW’s nitrogen sulphur product exclusively imported by ADM, is also available for delivery, although stocks are limited like other high nitrogen sulphur products on the UK market.
  • Potash and phosphate product demand has been significantly subdued in the UK given high prices. It remains unlikely we will see corrections before autumn PK application, but forward prices beyond that point remain uncertain.
