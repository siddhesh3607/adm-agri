Thursday 8th December 2022

Subscribe to the ADM Agriculture YouTube channel for the latest grain, seed & fertiliser market updates, and much more!

This week: Seed trader, Beth Moses, and seed assistant, Rosie Wray, discuss catch/cover cropping within an arable rotation.

Wheat

  • The bearish sentiment surrounding US wheat exports, contrasted by ongoing Black Sea trade, continues to weigh on US and global prices.
  • Chicago wheat has lost $16/t over the past week, at one point hitting a 14-month low.
  • Ukraine’s agriculture ministry reported that the country had exported almost 18.3mln t of grain so far this season. Although 30% down year-on-year, it is seen as a decent figure under the circumstances. Volumes including 6.9mln t of wheat, 9.8mln t of maize and 1.5mln t of barley.
  • Ukraine’s harvest totals 41.9mln t of grain from 85% of the expected area as of 1st December. Wheat and barley harvests are complete, with production put at  4mln t and 5.6mln t respectively.
  • Canada’s all-wheat production is put at 33.8mln t by the national statistics service, down from a previous estimate of 34.7mln t but still 52% higher year on year.
  • Australia is poised to harvest a record wheat crop of 36.6mln t this season, despite heavy rains affecting harvested area and yield in the eastern states.
  • Buenos Aires Grain Exchange expects dryness to delay maize plantings in Argentina, reported at only 25% complete as of 1st December. The wheat harvest is said to be 23% complete, with crop estimates at 12.4mln t.
  • Rosario Grain Exchange says Argentina’s wheat exports for 2022/23 could slip to just 6.5mln t, down from 14.5mln t last season and the lowest in eight years.
  • China is seen ramping up maize purchases from Brazil after shipments began in November, with reports that total exports may exceed 1mln t by the end of this year, replacing more expensive US supplies.
  • Soft wheat sowings in France were 99% complete as of 28th November, with 98% of the crop reported as being in either good or very good condition, similar to a year earlier.
  • EU soft wheat exports had reached 14.5mln t as of 4th December, compared with 14.0mln t by the same week last year. Algeria, Morocco and Egypt remain the top destinations.
  • Paris milling wheat prices fell to their lowest level this week since February, just after the war erupted in Ukraine, following Chicago’s decline.
  • UK prices have fallen just over £10/t week on week as the bearish global sentiment and weak domestic demand, mainly due to the bird-flu epidemic, continue to pressure futures and ex-farm prices.

Malting Barley

  • Malting barley markets have been slow again this week. Demand is completely disengaged and happy to watch prices come lower, both in the UK and abroad.
  • Farmer selling on malting barley is equally slow, despite premiums to feed still holding up and malting barley supply on paper looking plentiful.
  • We do not expect to see too much activity until the industry engages with the market in the New Year.

Feed Barley

    • Consumers in the UK market are still completely inactive. Cover remains high in the spot position, and confidence to put on new purchases is low going into the New Year with continued demand destruction looming over the market. Feed barley prices are narrowing the gap with wheat as futures continue their downtrend.
    • Stocks are high in Spain, maize is competitive in Ireland, and inland flows are undercutting UK replacement in the Netherlands, all in all creating a difficult market in which to find demand from our traditional European buyers.
    • Turkey’s TMO bought 495,000t of Jan/Feb shipments out of Russia/ex store stocks already in country, well below replacement levels from Northern Europe. Other than this there is very little demand from Third Country destinations.
    • Farmer selling in the UK is slow now across the board as we head into Christmas.

     

Rapeseed

    • CBOT Soybeans have traded higher on the week along with meal, but soy-oil is struggling to find support.
    • The trade will keep a watchful eye on South American weather developments in the coming weeks. Whilst Argentina now has some showers in the forecast for this coming weekend it may not be enough. Plantings are reported at just 29% vs 46% on average.
    • On Friday we will get updates from CONAB, BAGE and USDA. We should see export sales updates too; it will be interesting to assess the pick-up in China following some of the easing of Covid restrictions.
    • Chinese demand for US and SA soybeans has increased throughout the week, with several flash sales being reported.
    • Energy markets have pushed to nearby lows with OPEC leaving oil production unchanged.  Soy-oil pushed sharply lower this week, closing down six sessions in a row to levels not seen since 13 October. News of the lower-than-expected bio-fuel mandates are taking their toll in the short term.
    • In Canada, Stats Can released its latest crop estimate on Friday, at 18.2mln t vs 19.1mln t in its previous report. Other private estimates were lowered to 18.6mln t following lower yields in Alberta.
    • In Australia, ABARES also announced its latest canola crop estimate at 7.3mmt, despite rain in the east. Some private estimates are even closer to 8mln t.
    • Matif rapeseed is trading near the bottom of its recent range and a long way off recent highs. In the short-term the market looks well supplied with no significant increase in oil demand to give prices any support.

     

Oats

    • The oat market remains in a state of inertia with buyers largely absent. Some feed buying activity into near continent has seen UK oats trade above feed barley.
    • Supplies of generic feed oats are slim and prices now match milling oats, which exceed those for feed barley.
    • Demand into Spain continues with buyers providing indicative bids, but little seems to be trading. Finland remains too expensive and German millers are looking for UK and Swedish supplies to satisfy demand. The UK remains the cheapest source of oats and this could support domestic UK prices.
    • The UK milling oat trade remains slow with only small volumes trading. The rapid pace of exports is likely to support prices with close to 82,000t shipped to Europe as of 4 December. If this pace continues we will surpass last year’s level and could see UK supplies tighten at the end of the season.
    • Bottom line, the UK is the cheapest supplier of oats into Europe and this should support prices.

     

Pulses

Beans:

  • Old crop feed market consumers are effectively covered until the end of 2022 but we expect demand to pick up in the new year.
  • The human consumption market remains silent for the time being with Egypt still struggling for access to the US Dollar. We hope to see some demand in Q1.

 

Peas:

  • Feed pea demand remains slim until the new year, where in the first two quarters we expect to see some demand domestically. Exporters are well covered at the moment through other destinations.
  • Human consumption peas are in strong demand domestically and abroad, with marrowfat demand seeing another spike and large blue peas also seeing fresh demand.
  • Prices have remained at similar levels despite other commodities seeing a larger drop off. We expect this trend to continue into 2023.

 

Seed

  • ADM has marrowfat pea Kabuki and large blues Daytona and Bluetime seed available on market-leading buybacks.
  • For those looking to maximise wheat area, we have can offer spring wheat KWS Cochise.
  • ADM has a great portfolio of maize ranging from earlier maturing varieties to slightly later ones to best suit your needs. Whether you are looking for grain maize or whole crop, get in touch with your farm trader for more information.
  • Due to the limited supply of maize seed this year varieties are selling fast; it is advised to book as soon as possible to avoid disappointment.
  • Visit ADM Agriculture’s YouTube channel for our latest seed video, focusing on cover cropping within an arable rotation.

 

 

Fertiliser

  • International granular urea prices remain largely stable, although US values moved up by $15 FOB as buyers started to show increased interest.
  • ADM has granular urea options for the current season basis December – March delivery. Pricing remains very competitive on a cost per kg/N versus all other sources of nitrogen.
  • For those looking to manage input cost expectations for next season, ADM has a limited offer on granular urea on a POA basis.
  • Dutch front-month natural gas futures traded around €140/MWh, well above the six-month low of €97.8/MWh seen on 11 November, 2022. It remains to be seen what effect this could have on European AN prices.
  • The purchasing of autumn PKs appears well behind previous years. Demand destruction is evident in all markets, although with values now appearing to be workable, business has started to pick up in the UK.
